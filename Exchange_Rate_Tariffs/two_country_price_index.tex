\section*{Two-Country, Two-Sector, One-Factor Model}

\section*{1. Environment}

We consider two countries, Home (H) and Foreign (F). 
Each country produces:
\begin{itemize}
    \item a country-specific \textbf{tradable good} (differentiated by origin),
    \item a \textbf{non-tradable good} (consumed only domestically).
\end{itemize}

\section*{2. Household Preferences}

A representative household in country $i \in \{H,F\}$ maximizes:
\[
U_i = E_0 \sum_{t=0}^{\infty} \beta^t \, u(C_{i,t}, N_{i,t}).
\]



Final composite consumption: 
\[
C_{i}=\left[ (1-\alpha )C_{T_{i}}^{\frac{\sigma -1}{\sigma }}+\alpha
C_{N_{i}}^{\frac{\sigma -1}{\sigma }}\right] ^{\frac{\sigma }{\sigma -1}}, 
\]%
where $\sigma $ is the elasticity of substitution between tradables and
non-tradables.

\subsection*{2.1 Tradable Sub-Basket}

Tradables from Home and Foreign are imperfect substitutes: 
\[
C_{T_{i}}=\left[ (1-\omega )C_{H_{i}}^{\frac{\eta -1}{\eta }}+\omega
C_{F_{i}}^{\frac{\eta -1}{\eta }}\right] ^{\frac{\eta }{\eta -1}}, 
\]%
where $\eta $ is the elasticity of substitution between foreign and domestic
tradable goods.

\section*{3. Prices}

\subsection*{3.1 Final Consumption Price Index}

\[
P_{i}=\left[ (1-\alpha )P_{T_{i}}^{1-\sigma }+\alpha P_{N_{i}}^{1-\sigma }%
\right] ^{\frac{1}{1-\sigma }}. 
\]

\subsection*{3.2 Tradable Price Index}

\[
P_{T_{i}}=\left[ (1-\omega )P_{H_{i}}^{1-\eta }+\omega P_{F_{i}}^{1-\eta }%
\right] ^{\frac{1}{1-\eta }}. 
\]
